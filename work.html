All These Things That I've Done 🎵

I won Best Overall Individual at BT Young Scientist 2023 -- Ireland's National Science Fair -- for my project on computational analysis to identify key signalling pathways implicated pathological phenotypes in breast cancer, and develop data-driven models to reverse those states. For this I was featured on the front of Connected Magazine as one of Ireland's 30 under 30, dubbed "One of the brightest and most innovative minds this island has to offer".


I am the creator of Higher-Level Oracle Description Language (HODL). This is a passion project of mine I started working on 3.5 years ago that makes it super easy and intuitive to express classical logic/arithmetic within quantum circuits. For this I was awarded a grant by the Unitary Fund and won the Technology Category at BTYSTE 2021.

...and have experimented with exposing a custom QC simulator to a hobby OS kernel -- fun stuff that took the better part of a year. Not of much practical value, but was really stimulating and kinda difficult at the time and I liked that, particularly treasuring the moments spent coding in x86 assembly. I was working with shared memory systems, implementing DMA transfers, filsystems, classical/quantum process management (each classical process has a corresponding quantum process control block) etc. I also wrote a scripting language for the system called Q++ with which I wrote a rudimentary shell program, and a proof-of-concept VQE algorithm implementation.
