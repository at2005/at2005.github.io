On Q

Whenever I get asked why I began work on Q, I tend to say stuff like, "Oh, well certain aspects of quantum algorithms can be quite hard to code by hand in today's gate-based environment", and then go off on a whole explanation of oracles and classical functions, and then how Q provides a more user-friendly way to implement them.

To be completely honest, though, that's not really why I ever decided to make it. Sure, it felt great to say that Q could do some things better than other languages, but it's not like I went, "Hey, I want to make it easier to code oracles. Let's spend the entire year trying to do that!".

No, the main reason is because it seemed like an interesting thing to do. I like building things from the ground up. I don't know why, but even the thought of extending software, triggers unfavourable chemical responses up there. If someone were to hand over the entire codebase for say, Facebook, then I can pretty much guarantee you'll see me working on a re-implementation. That's also why the option of extending QISKit seemed so bizarre. It's not that I didn't want to, it's just, I prefer building new things, and the concept that I could build my own language seemed much more exciting than writing some library.
